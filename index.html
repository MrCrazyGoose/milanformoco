About Milan
An organizer,
not a politician

I'm running for Democratic Central Committee in District 15 to build strong, year-round grassroots organizing infrastructure — and to stand up for our immigrant and marginalized communities against the Trump Administration.

As a child of immigrants, I know what it means to fight on the ground, not from a podium. I've helped run one of the largest nationwide grassroots Democratic organizations, organized campaigns and initiatives across Montgomery County and the state, and advocated for increased accessibility and accountability within the state party and the DNC.

"We all know these are not ordinary times. We need change, and a new generation of leadership to move the party forward and fight for our county."

— Milan van Dam

The Platform
Fighting for a party that…

Four commitments. Real action. Built for this moment.

Jump to pillar
Organizes Everywhere, Year-Round
We cannot leave any community behind, any time unused.
  • Invest in year-round field work in District 15 and countywide, working with precinct leaders and clubs.
  • Prioritize precinct-level and club-based organizing and building strong volunteer pipelines.
  • Establish campaign training programs, organize midterm phonebanks and canvasses.
Stands Up to Trump & MAGA
Our communities are under attack. We have to fight back.
  • Build digital rapid response capacity and rebuild the central committee's social media presence.
  • Unite organizations, communities, and campaigns against Trump's actions.
  • Make the local party a force for action — from protests to phonebanks.
Builds a Party for the People
More representative, focused on delivering real results.
  • Move towards special elections from the appointment system.
  • Host regular town halls, record meetings, and open a feedback form for the party.
  • Show up to local events and maintain regular communication with community leaders.
Defends Communities & Rejects Hate
A party that reflects our diversity and protects the vulnerable.
  • Stand up for immigrant communities with consistent advocacy and presence.
  • Reject antisemitism and hate clearly and consistently.
  • Work closely with clubs to ensure all voices are heard — including high school, college, and young Democrats.
